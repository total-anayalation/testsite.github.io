About Me

CS meets data science meets education policy — the through-line is using rigorous tools to solve problems that actually matter.

Hi! I'm Anaya, a sophomore at the University of Wisconsin–Madison studying Computer Science, Data Science, and Education Policy — yes, all three, and yes, they connect more than you'd think. I'm fascinated by how technology shapes institutions, and how institutions shape who gets access to technology.

I'm an active member of UW's AI Safety organization, where I spend a lot of time thinking about alignment, interpretability, and what it actually means to build AI systems that are robust and beneficial. I believe AI safety is one of the most important problems of our generation — and I think people with policy and social science backgrounds need to be in that conversation.

The intersection

My academic interests live at the crossroads of technical rigor and human impact. In CS, I love machine learning and systems. In data science, I gravitate toward causal inference and fairness. In education policy, I care about how evidence actually (or doesn't) make it into decisions. Put those together and you get someone who really enjoys a good messy problem.

Outside class

When I'm not studying or at an AI safety reading group, you might find me at a coffee shop with a book, exploring Madison's lakes, or going way too deep on some random Wikipedia rabbit hole. I believe in warm conversations, intellectual honesty, and the power of a good question.
